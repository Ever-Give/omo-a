"Be what?" Del could barely hear her own voice, and hoped she wasn't yelling.

"Be," Alvin enunciated. Del didn't understand until he gestured to his crotch.

"Oh." Del tried for a half-whisper. "Pee. Go ahead." She nodded. Alvin faced the cliff next to her. It threw his front into shadow so she could only see vague movement and she couldn't hear him yet, but he gave a long, silent sigh of relief that Del felt in her bones. Del left when she finished to rinse her shoes in the lake, and then it was time to go.
